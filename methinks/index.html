Things to notice

Crossover's modest edge

Race them and crossover wins, but only by roughly half — not the landslide you might expect. The weasel target is separable: every character is its own tiny building block, so there's little structure for recombination to exploit.

The hill-climber's hidden cost

In the Lab its curve rockets upward with population size. A population of hill-climbers re-tests every member each generation, so its fitness-evaluation bill balloons while the GAs stay lean.

The mutation sweet spot

Push the mutation slider far either way and convergence stalls. Too little and errors take forever to fix; too much and selection can't hold onto a correct character — the error catastrophe.
