The thread that connects them

The weasel shows selection crushing blind chance — but its letters are independent, so recombination only helps a little. HIFF is the mirror image: a problem built from nested modules where crossover is essential and a mutation-only search gets stuck. Fitness landscapes give the why — ruggedness and deception are what separate the two. Together they trace the course's big idea: recombination earns its keep exactly when a problem has reusable building blocks to combine.

Empedocles — named for the pre-Socratic philosopher who, around 450 BC, imagined organisms assembled from randomly combined parts with only the viable surviving: an ancient glimpse of natural selection. Coursework from the University of Southampton MSc in Artificial Intelligence.
